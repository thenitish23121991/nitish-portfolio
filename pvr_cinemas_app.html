Designing the mobile experience for the largest cinemas chain in India
PVR Cinemas App
Dec 2018 - Feb 2019
I worked as the product designer and later as the lead designer for some key features of the PVR Cinemas app. Worked alongside the Ecommerce Head, PM, engineers and other stakeholders to design features allowing users to effortlessly book their movie tickets with a touch of delight.
About PVR Cinemas
PVR Cinemas
PVR Ltd. is the largest and the most premium film exhibition company in India. Since its inception in 1997, the brand has redefined the cinema industry and the way people watch movies in the country. The company acquired Cinemax in 2012 and had taken over DT Cinemas in the year 2016 serving 100 million + patrons annually. Currently PVR operates a cinema circuit of 795+ Screens 165+ Properties 65+ Cities (21 states and 1 Union Territory).
The Homepage
Redesigning the homepage
The homepage for the PVR Cinemas app was the most critical page on the app. Based on the changes in our business logic, we needed to prioritize the PVR Privilege program while making sure that it does not get in the way of the primary goal of the app, which is booking movie tickets.
The Old Homepage
The Hypothesis
The obvious solution was to move the widget to the top of the feed. While doing so, we also did not want it to feel too overbearing. We worked on a couple of variations, one with a reduced size but still having the CTA button and another without the CTA button. Our hypothesis behind creating the variation with no CTA was that using an effective copy would act as a CTA itself. This would also help in the “Book” CTA on the featured movie to stand out more since it is not competing with other CTAs.
The New Homepage
Implementing the new solution
The PVR Privilege widget was too large and drew too much attention away from booking movie tickets, which was the app's primary function. We positioned the PVR Privilege widget at the top, removed the CTA since the language acted as a signifier and it did not overshadow the movie cards.

Gathering User Research Insights
We had research insights that showed us that users generally did not scroll past the first 5-6 results and while getting the information related to the cinema was important to them, they would often want to know if the movies that interest them were playing at those cinemas.

The New Solution
One of the immediate problem areas we realised in our initial user tests were users assumed that the movie names in the "Movies Playing" section were clickable, while they were not, they just showed the user which movies were playing in that cinema during that time. Digesting information about a cinema also wasn't easy for the users.
Some variations we tried for the Cinemas Page
Testing variations we created
The card groups similar information together. The inset seperator acts as a visual differentiator between the two sets of information. We A/B tested the variations with users to see which variant performed the best for a week. The results collected from the tests validated our hypothesis and we went ahead with the first variant.

Designing Prebooking Feature
Prebook your movies well before they release
The prebooking feature of the PVR Cinemas app allows users to prebook tickets for a movie before a movie is even released. You can also select upto 3 of your favourite cinemas to book tickets at.
Brief
The Brief for designing the Prebooking feature was to make sure the process is simple, it must create a sense of anticipation amongst the user and it must fit in nicely with the already used design patterns.
The Solution
The Prebooking flow was designed to make sure that the with the help of the movie cover, the design displays the grandness and modernity of the experience of watching movies in a PVR theatre. The transparent status bar as well as the top navigation bar along with carefully picked movie covers helped us in acheiving that. The movie covers were used for devices of different sizes in such a way that the subject of the movie cover is never clipped and always stands out in the picture.
